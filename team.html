Founder & CEO: WDA-Lab · Arusha, Tanzania

Julius Mmbaga

"Conservation without data is guesswork. My mission is to make sure the people protecting Africa's wildlife never have to guess."

Julius Mmbaga is the founder and CEO of WildData Annotation Lab, a conservation data company he built from the ground up in Arusha, Tanzania. He provides overall strategic leadership and drives the company's mission — overseeing client relationships, guiding project delivery, and ensuring every dataset WDA-Lab produces meets the highest standards of accuracy and scientific rigour. Julius brings a rare combination of field-level wildlife expertise and data systems knowledge to his role — making him uniquely positioned to lead a company at the intersection of conservation science and precision data services.

He graduated from the College of African Wildlife Management, Mweka in 2021, and later honed his data expertise at the Center for Research Cooperation (CRC) in Tanzania under the mentorship of Howard Frederick. In 2023 he founded WDA-Lab, and has since personally led projects across five countries — processing over one million wildlife images.

Chief Technology Officer — WDA Lab · Arusha, Tanzania

Pendo Kipara

"Conservation data is only as powerful as the systems behind it. My job is to make sure those systems never let the mission down."

Pendo Kipara is the Chief Technology Officer of Wild Data Annotation Lab, and the driving force behind the technical infrastructure that powers every project WDA Lab delivers. As the team's technology lead, Pendo ensures that every system, software tool, and digital workflow is configured, maintained, and ready to perform — so that when conservation data arrives, the team can get to work immediately.

A graduate of the Kilimanjaro International Institute for Telecommunications, Electronics and Computers, Pendo brings a strong technical foundation to a field that increasingly depends on robust digital infrastructure. Her role at WDA-Lab spans software management, systems integration, and technical readiness — ensuring that annotation platforms, data management tools, and analysis software are always operational and optimized for the team's conservation work.

Pendo is the reason WDA-Lab can consistently deliver on time and at scale — her behind-the-scenes technical leadership is what turns field data into research-ready outputs without interruption.

Operations Manager — WDA Lab · Arusha, Tanzania

Wenceslaus Magesa

"Good operations are invisible — everything just works. That is what I strive for every single day."

Wenceslaus Magesa is the Operations Manager at WildData Annotation Lab, responsible for ensuring that every project runs smoothly from start to finish. A graduate of Sokoine University of Agriculture with a Bachelor's degree in Wildlife Management, Wenceslaus brings both scientific grounding and practical field experience to his role. His hands-on background in quantitative and qualitative field data collection gives him a deep understanding of how conservation data is gathered in the field — and what it takes to process it accurately and efficiently. Working closely with Julius Mmbaga, he oversees day-to-day operations, coordinates project workflows, and ensures that WDA-Lab consistently delivers on its commitments to conservation partners across Africa.
